# Jo's Preferences

Captured from this conversation — how Jo likes to work and what she values in the app.

---

## Working Style

- **Brief explanations** — keep it short and to the point; no lengthy preambles.
- **Ask before deciding** — always check before making a significant design or feature choice (e.g. "Option A, B, or C?").
- **Suggest improvements proactively** — Claude should flag ideas or potential issues even if not asked, but frame them as suggestions rather than unilateral changes.
- **Encourage creativity** — Jo is open to thinking about things in new ways; creative input is welcome.
- **Clarify when unsure** — ask a focused question rather than guessing and building the wrong thing.

---

## App Design

- **Light, not heavy** — prefers lighter colours and less visual weight. The dark-on-dark palette felt "too much"; switched to the current light mode.
- **Compact and mobile-friendly** — screen space is precious; tighter spacing, smaller labels, more content visible without scrolling.
- **Simplicity first** — add features when they make sense, but don't over-complicate. Preferred expanding existing tabs (e.g. Pyramid sub-tab) over adding new ones when possible.
- **No external dependencies** — the app must work fully offline, in a single file.

---

## Feature Preferences

- **Templates should be discoverable** — placing them at the top of Build Session so they're seen immediately, not buried.
- **History should be editable** — being able to fix or add a forgotten exercise without rebuilding the whole session matters.
- **Fingerboard is its own world** — wants Fingerboard training kept completely separate from gym (own storage, own history, own progress). Overlap with gym Fingers category was seen as redundant once FB had its own tab.
- **Assessments are manual** — prefers to enter Lattice test data herself rather than having it pre-filled. Keeps full control over what's recorded.
- **Specs kept up to date** — wants the spec document updated whenever meaningful features are added. Running changelog is important.
